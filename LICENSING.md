# Licensing (Keel)

This document is a plain-language summary of Keel’s licensing intent.
It is not a legal document. The full text in `LICENSE` governs in all cases.

## Current licence

Keel is licensed under the Business Source License 1.1 (BSL 1.1). BSL grants everyone the
right to copy, modify, create derivative works, redistribute, and make non-production use of the
Licensed Work.

Production use is permitted only via the Additional Use Grant in `LICENSE`, or with an active
commercial licence from the Licensor.

Commercial licences are available at:
https://thresholdsignalworks.com/cloud

## Primary goal (anti-clone protection)

The primary goal of using BSL for Keel is to prevent third parties from forking Keel and offering a
competing hosted or managed service, or embedding Keel into a commercial product offering, without
a commercial licence from the original author.

## What counts as production use (anti-clone focus)

If you do any of the following, you are making production use and you need a commercial licence:

- Offering Keel (or a fork/derivative) as a hosted service or managed service to third parties.
- Embedding Keel into a commercial product or service where customers receive Keel functionality as
  part of that offering (including “Keel-as-a-feature” inside a broader platform).
- Reselling Keel functionality, whether directly or via a wrapper, gateway, proxy, or plugin.

If you want to build a product or service on top of Keel, contact Threshold Signalworks or obtain a
commercial licence via the Cloud plans.

This list is illustrative, not exhaustive. The Additional Use Grant in `LICENSE` governs the full scope of permitted production use

## Additional Use Grant (free uses)

Per the Additional Use Grant in `LICENSE`, you may make production use of the Licensed Work for:

- (a) personal, non-commercial projects; and
- (b) academic teaching and non-commercial research, provided the Licensed Work is cited in any
  resulting publication.

Note: university IT departments managing institutional infrastructure (rather than teaching or
non-commercial research) should treat that as commercial use.
